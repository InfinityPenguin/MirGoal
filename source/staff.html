  • Wenzhe Li

    VP of R&D and Business Development, NMS LABS

    Wenzhe Li, Ph.D., DABCC, MBA

    VP of R&D and Business Development, NMS LABS

    Dr. Wenzhe Li has served as Chairperson of the board of directors at MirGoal since March 2015. Dr. Li has been the Medical Officer of Global Medical Affairs of Siemens Healthcare Diagnostics, a division of Siemens AG, a $9.4 billion revenue international company, since 2007. Previously, Dr. Li has held key technical and managerial positions at DPC (Diagnostic Product Corporation), a publicly traded diagnostics company focused on immunoassay platforms and reagents, and laboratory of Harborview Hospital, a University of Washington Laboratory Medicine affiliated 600 beds trauma center hospital. She is also the Vice President of R&D and Business Development, and Executive Team member for NMS Labs, a topline private specialty national reference laboratory of 40 years history, located in Pennsylvania. Dr. Li received a Ph.D. in virology from the University of Nebraska-Lincoln, and completed her postdoctoral fellowship training in Laboratory Medicine at the University of Washington-Seattle. Dr. Li's MBA was acquired at The Paul Merage School of Business, University of California, Irvine. Dr. Li is a board certified CLIA laboratory director by the ABCC (American Board of Clinical Chemistry).

  • Jingfang Ju

    Co-Director of Translational Research/Associate Professor, Department of Pathology, Stony Brook Medicine, Stony Brook University

    Jingfang Ju, Ph.D.

    Co-Director of Translational Research/Associate Professor, Department of Pathology, Stony Brook Medicine, Stony Brook University

    Dr. Jingfang Ju is the Co-Director of the Translational Research Laboratory in the Department of Pathology at Stony Brook Medicine/Stony Brook University. Dr. Ju's major interest is in the development of microRNA based therapeutics and biomarker in gastrointestinal cancer. The Ju laboratory studies the mechanisms of microRNAs in cancer stem cell resistance, EMT, autophagy, and apoptosis. His group made initial discovery of the regulatory relationship between p53 tumor suppressor and microRNA. Dr. Ju and his group discovered the superior stability of microRNA in archival formalin fixed paraffin embedded (FFPE) clinical specimens which serves as the foundation of microRNA based biomarker discovery. Dr. Ju received his bachelor's degree from the Northeastern University and Ph.D. in molecular biology and biochemistry at the University of Southern California. Dr. Ju completed his post-doctoral research fellowshop at Yale University. Previously Dr. Ju has served as the Senior Scientist and Team Leader at CuraGen Corporation in New Haven, Connecticut.
